The Art of Tatreez

More than just clothing, Palestinian embroidery is a language of identity, resistance, and generational storytelling.

Every stitch carries the history of the land. Historically, the colors and patterns of a thobe indicated the wearer's region, marital status, and social standing. We work directly with female artisans to preserve this beautiful tradition while adapting it for the modern wardrobe, ensuring fair wages and cultural preservation.

Caring for Your Heritage

Traditional Palestinian embroidery requires gentle care to preserve the vibrant threads and delicate fabrics for generations to come.

We highly recommend hand washing your thobes in cold water with a mild, pH-neutral detergent. Avoid bleach, fabric softeners, or harsh chemicals.

Always air dry your garments by laying them flat on a clean, dry towel in the shade. Never wring or twist the fabric to remove water.

When ironing, always turn the garment inside out. Use a low-heat setting and a pressing cloth to protect both the base fabric and the embroidery.
